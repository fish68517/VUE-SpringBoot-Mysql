## Creating Training Plans

### Understanding Training Plans

A training plan is a structured fitness program you create for a specific student. It includes:
- **Plan Name**: Descriptive title (e.g., "8-Week Strength Building")
- **Description**: Overview of the plan's goals
- **Exercises**: Specific workouts with details
- **Duration**: Start and end dates
- **Status**: Active, Completed, or Cancelled

### Creating a New Plan

1. Click **Create Plan** in the sidebar
2. Fill in the plan information:
   - **Student**: Select from your student list
   - **Plan Name**: Give it a descriptive name
   - **Description**: Explain the plan's goals and approach
   - **Start Date**: When the plan begins
   - **End Date**: When the plan ends

3. Add exercises:
   - Click **Add Exercise**
   - Enter exercise details:
     - **Exercise Name**: (e.g., "Bench Press")
     - **Sets**: Number of sets
     - **Reps**: Repetitions per set
     - **Duration**: Time per set (in minutes) or weight
   - Click **Add** to add the exercise
   - Repeat for all exercises

4. Click **Create Plan**
5. The plan is assigned to the student
6. The student receives a notification

### Editing a Training Plan

1. Go to **Students** and select a student
2. Find the training plan to edit
3. Click the **Edit** button
4. Modify the plan details:
   - Name, description, dates
   - Add, remove, or modify exercises
5. Click **Save**
6. Changes are applied immediately

### Deleting a Training Plan

1. Go to **Students** and select a student
2. Find the training plan to delete
3. Click the **Delete** button
4. Confirm the deletion
5. The plan is removed (historical data is preserved)

### Plan Status Management

**Changing Plan Status:**
1. Open the training plan
2. Click the **Status** dropdown
3. Select: Active, Completed, or Cancelled
4. Click **Save**

**Status Meanings:**
- **Active**: Student is currently following this plan
- **Completed**: Plan has been finished successfully
- **Cancelled**: Plan was discontinued

## Publishing Fitness Content

### Content Types

You can publish three types of fitness content:
- **Videos**: Fitness tutorials and demonstrations
- **Articles**: Written guides and tips
- **Documents**: PDFs and downloadable materials

### Creating Video Content

1. Click **My Content** in the sidebar
2. Click **Create Content**
3. Select **Video** as content type
4. Fill in the details:
   - **Title**: Descriptive title
   - **Description**: Overview of the video
   - **Video File**: Upload MP4 or AVI (max 100MB)
5. Click **Publish**
6. The video is available to all users

### Creating Article Content

1. Click **My Content** in the sidebar
2. Click **Create Content**
3. Select **Article** as content type
4. Fill in the details:
   - **Title**: Article title
   - **Description**: Brief summary
   - **Content**: Write your article text
5. Click **Publish**
6. The article is available to all users

### Creating Document Content

1. Click **My Content** in the sidebar
2. Click **Create Content**
3. Select **Document** as content type
4. Fill in the details:
   - **Title**: Document title
   - **Description**: What the document contains
   - **Document File**: Upload PDF or other document (max 100MB)
5. Click **Publish**
6. The document is available to all users

### Managing Your Content

## Viewing Student Analytics

### Accessing Analytics

1. Click **Analytics** in the sidebar
2. Select a student from the list
3. View their comprehensive analytics

### Understanding Analytics Metrics

**Check-In Frequency:**
- Shows how consistently the student is checking in
- Displayed as a chart over the last 30 days
- Helps identify commitment and consistency

**Total Check-Ins:**
- Cumulative number of check-ins
- Indicates overall engagement

**Average Daily Calories:**
- Calculated from diet records
- Shows nutritional patterns
- Helps assess diet consistency

**Active Days:**
- Number of days with activity (check-ins or diet logs)
- Indicates engagement level

**Plan Completion Rate:**
- Percentage of assigned plans completed
- Shows adherence to training programs

### Analyzing Student Progress

**Identifying Trends:**
1. Review the check-in chart for patterns
2. Look for consistency or gaps
3. Note seasonal variations

**Comparing Metrics:**
1. Compare current metrics to previous periods
2. Identify improvements or declines
3. Adjust plans based on trends

**Taking Action:**
- If check-ins are declining, reach out to the student
- If diet is inconsistent, provide nutritional guidance
- If plans aren't completed, discuss challenges

### Exporting Analytics

1. Open a student's analytics
2. Click **Export** button
3. Choose format: PDF or CSV
4. The report downloads to your computer

## Best Practices

### For Training Plans

1. **Be Specific**: Include clear exercise names, sets, and reps
2. **Set Realistic Timelines**: Give students adequate time to complete plans
3. **Progressive Overload**: Gradually increase difficulty in subsequent plans
4. **Variety**: Mix different types of exercises to prevent boredom
5. **Communication**: Discuss plans with students before assigning

### For Content Creation

1. **Quality Over Quantity**: Focus on high-quality, useful content
2. **Clear Instructions**: Make videos and articles easy to follow
3. **Consistency**: Maintain a regular publishing schedule
4. **Relevance**: Create content that addresses common student needs
5. **Updates**: Keep content current and accurate

### For Student Management

1. **Regular Check-Ins**: Monitor student progress weekly
2. **Feedback**: Provide constructive feedback on performance
3. **Motivation**: Celebrate milestones and achievements
4. **Flexibility**: Adjust plans based on student feedback
5. **Documentation**: Keep records of student progress

### For Analytics Review

1. **Weekly Reviews**: Check student analytics regularly
2. **Identify Issues Early**: Address declining engagement promptly
3. **Celebrate Success**: Recognize students meeting goals
4. **Data-Driven Decisions**: Use analytics to inform plan adjustments
5. **Personalization**: Tailor guidance based on individual metrics

## FAQ

### Student Management

**Q: Can I have unlimited students?**
A: Yes, there's no limit to the number of students you can coach.

**Q: What happens if a student leaves?**
A: You can remove them from your roster. Their training plans remain in the system for reference.

**Q: Can students see each other's information?**
A: No, student information is private. Each student only sees their own data.

### Training Plans

**Q: Can I create plans for multiple students at once?**
A: No, plans are created individually. You can duplicate a plan for similar students.

**Q: Can students modify their training plans?**
A: No, only coaches can modify plans. Students can view and follow them.

**Q: How do I know if a student completed a plan?**
A: Check their analytics and manually update the plan status to "Completed" when done.

### Content & Analytics

**Q: How long does it take for published content to appear?**
A: Content is published immediately and available to all users.

**Q: Can I see who viewed my content?**
A: You can see the total view count, but not individual viewer information.

**Q: How far back does analytics data go?**
A: Analytics show data for the last 30 days by default. Historical data is preserved.

**Q: Can I compare multiple students' analytics?**
A: Currently, you view one student at a time. You can manually compare by viewing each student's analytics.

### Technical Issues

**Q: What file formats are supported for videos?**
A: MP4 and AVI formats are supported (max 100MB).

**Q: What file formats are supported for documents?**
A: PDF and common document formats are supported (max 100MB).

**Q: Can I schedule content to publish later?**
A: No, content is published immediately upon creation.
